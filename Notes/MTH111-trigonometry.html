MATHEMATICAL INDUCTION

Trigonometry is the study of triangles, particularly triangles with right angles (90 degrees).
It involves the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. Basic Concepts: - _Angles_: measured in degrees, minutes, and seconds - _Triangles_: classified as acute, right, obtuse, or straight - _Trigonometric Ratios_: relationships between the sides of a right triangle Trigonometric Ratios: - _Sine (sin)_ : opposite side / hypotenuse - _Cosine (cos)_ : adjacent side / hypotenuse - _Tangent (tan)_ : opposite side / adjacent side - _Cosecant (cosec)_ : hypotenuse / opposite side - _Secant (sec)_ : hypotenuse / adjacent side - _Cotangent (cot)_ : adjacent side / opposite side Trigonometric Identities: - _Pythagorean Identity_ : sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1 - _Sum and Difference Identities_ : sin(a+b) = sin(a)cos(b) + cos(a)sin(b), etc. - _Double Angle Identities_ : sin(2x) = 2sin(x)cos(x), etc. Graphs of Trigonometric Functions: - _Sine and Cosine Graphs_ : periodic graphs with amplitude and period - _Tangent Graph_ : periodic graph with vertical asymptotes Applications of Trigonometry: - _Navigation and Surveying_ : calculating distances and angles in navigation and surveying - _Physics and Engineering_ : modeling periodic phenomena, such as sound waves and light waves - _Computer Graphics_ : creating 3D models and animations Examples: - Evaluate the expression: sin(30°) + cos(60°) - Prove the identity: sin(a+b) = sin(a)cos(b) + cos(a)sin(b) - Find the equation of the graph: y = 2sin(x) + 1 Tips for Working with Trigonometry: - Understand the definitions of trigonometric ratios and identities. - Practice solving problems involving trigonometric functions and identities. - Visualize and graph trigonometric functions to understand their behavior.
