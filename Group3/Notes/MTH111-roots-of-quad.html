ROOTS OF QUADRATIC

A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of degree two, which can be written in the form ax^2 + bx + c = 0, where a, b, and c are constants.
The roots of a quadratic equation are the values of x that satisfy the equation. Methods for Finding Roots: - _Factoring Method_: factor the quadratic expression into two binomials, and then set each binomial equal to zero to find the roots. - _Quadratic Formula Method_: use the quadratic formula x = (-b ± √(b^2 - 4ac)) / 2a to find the roots. - _Graphical Method_: graph the quadratic function and find the x-intercepts, which represent the roots. Types of Roots: - _Real and Distinct Roots_: two real and distinct values of x that satisfy the equation. - _Real and Repeated Roots_: two real and equal values of x that satisfy the equation. - _Complex Roots_: two complex values of x that satisfy the equation. Quadratic Formula: - _x = (-b ± √(b^2 - 4ac)) / 2a_: the quadratic formula used to find the roots of a quadratic equation. - _Discriminant (b^2 - 4ac)_: the expression under the square root in the quadratic formula, which determines the nature of the roots. Examples: - Find the roots of the quadratic equation x^2 + 5x + 6 = 0 using the factoring method. - Use the quadratic formula to find the roots of the quadratic equation x^2 - 3x - 4 = 0. Tips for Working with Quadratic Equations: - Check if the quadratic expression can be factored easily. - Use the quadratic formula when factoring is not possible. - Always check the discriminant to determine the nature of the roots.
