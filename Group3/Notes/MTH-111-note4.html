COMPLEX NUMBERS

Complex numbers are numbers that can be expressed in the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers and i is the imaginary unit, satisfying i^2 = -1. Definition and Notation: - _Complex Number_: a number of the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers - _Real Part_: the real number a in the complex number a + bi - _Imaginary Part_: the real number b in the complex number a + bi - _Modulus_: the distance of the complex number from the origin, denoted as |a + bi| - _Argument_: the angle between the positive real axis and the line segment connecting the origin to the complex number Operations on Complex Numbers: - _Addition_: (a + bi) + (c + di) = (a + c) + (b + d)i - _Subtraction_: (a + bi) - (c + di) = (a - c) + (b - d)i - _Multiplication_: (a + bi)(c + di) = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i - _Division_: (a + bi)/(c + di) = ((ac + bd)/(c^2 + d^2)) + ((bc - ad)/(c^2 + d^2))i - _Conjugate_: the complex number a - bi is the conjugate of a + bi - _Modulus and Argument_: the modulus and argument of a complex number can be used to represent it in polar form Applications of Complex Numbers: - _Algebra and Geometry_: complex numbers are used to solve algebraic equations and represent geometric transformations - _Calculus and Analysis_: complex numbers are used to extend the concepts of calculus and analysis to complex-valued functions - _Physics and Engineering_: complex numbers are used to represent and analyze AC circuits, signals, and systems Examples: - Simplify the expression: (2 + 3i)(4 - 5i) - Find the modulus and argument of the complex number: 3 + 4i - Use complex numbers to solve the equation: x^2 + 4 = 0 Tips for Working with Complex Numbers: - Understand the definition and notation of complex numbers. - Practice performing operations on complex numbers, such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. - Use the properties of complex numbers, such as conjugates and modulus, to simplify expressions and solve equations.
